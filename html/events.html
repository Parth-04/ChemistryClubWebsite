Events

Events

We pride ourselves on hosting a plethora of engaging events throughout the year. Our events are not just gatherings, they are immersive experiences filled with experiments, collaboration, teamwork, and the joy of exploring diverse realms of chemistry and chemical engineering. From hands-on experiments that ignite the spark of discovery to collaborative initiatives that emphasize the power of teamwork, each event is meticulously crafted to provide participants with a dynamic and enriching experience. Join us on this journey of discovery as we delve into the fascinating world of chemistry and chemical engineering together!

Recent Events

Heisenberg is our club orientation organised every year for freshers to introduce them to various club activities and events. Students participated in a team of 3. Heisenberg involved various fun games and quizzes, mind-boggling riddles, and exciting prizes for the winning teams!

Reactorious is our project series launched by the club so that students can learn practical applications of all chemical and technical knowledge. Students were required to work on an idea under the professor’s guidance, ranging from optimised reactors to the concentration of microbes in drinking water. Including various hot fields like chemical sciences, nanotech, biosciences, energy sciences, etc.

The 3D Printer Workshop was organised by the club to teach the students about how to handle and use the 3D printer.

In this challenge, students were required to design a safe egg landing device, put an egg in it and make it have a free fall. The challenge was to keep the egg unbroken. The device should be capable of protecting the egg.

Winter School of Chemistry is our flagship Pan – India event conducted in the month of December. Winter school includes various courses in chemistry taught by students. Winter School is the perfect opportunity to dive deep into the rapidly developing interdisciplinary fields of chemistry. Various courses such as biochemistry, numerical methods, computational chemistry, and nanoparticle synthesis are taught.
